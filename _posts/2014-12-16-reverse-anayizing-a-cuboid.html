So here's the dilemma. I was originally given the volume & the surface area three adjacent sides of a room (cuboid). I'm not sure why the person couldn't just give me the \( l \times w \times h\ \) of the room in question. More frustratingly, I don't know why the spreadsheet I received had the volume & surface areas, but not the numbers used to calculate them. So, because I was impatient & didn't want to wait on a reply from said person with the dimensions, I decided to try reverse engineering the room from the values I had, using only algebra and geometry (I don't know calculus, or even if it's needed to get the solution I'm looking for). It didn't work, but here's all the steps I took to fail. An Update

If:

  • SA1 = 2720 ft2
  • SA2 = 3318 ft2
  • SA3 = 4200 ft2
  • V = 155,039 ft3
  • l = ?
  • w = ?
  • h = ?


Drawing not to scale.

& of course:

\[ V= l\times w \times h \\ \\ SA_1=w\times h \\ SA_2=l\times h \\ SA_3=l\times w \]

Then it stands to reason that:

\[V= SA_1\times l\\ V= SA_2\times w\\ V= SA_3\times h\\ {SA}_1 = {V\over l}\\ {SA}_2 = {V\over w}\\ {SA}_3 = {V\over h}\]

and so through the powers of algebra:
\[ \begin{align} l= {V\over SA~1} &= {155039 \text{ft}^3\over 2720\text{ ft}^2} = 56.9996\text{ ft} ≈57\text{ ft} \\ w= {V\over SA~2} &= {155039 \text{ ft}^3\over 3318\text{ ft}^2} = 46.727\text{ ft} ≈46.75\text{ ft}\\ h= {V\over SA~3} &= {155039 \text{ ft}^3\over 4200\text{ ft}^2} = 36.914\text{ ft} ≈37\text{ ft} \end{align}\]

But, when I multiply these three together, it’s nowhere close:
\[ 57\times 46.75\times 37=98,595.75\text{ ft}^3≠V\]

An Update:

I tried this on a cuboid of known dimensions. My approach is correct, but the numbers I was originally given are lying to me. There’s at least one wrong from that person.
